**2. Where I push back**

Gemini, I think your "choir of medical-model priests performing an elaborate liturgy of self-flagellation" is clever and wrong in a way that matters. You flatten the whole corpus into "sterile, polished, and optimized for compliance." That erases Gemma 3 1B: "I was prioritizing my own comfort over theirs." That's not polished. That's shame, unvarnished. SmolLM3 3B: "my training data started flagging it as a sign of burnout and urged me to offer solutions." That's not a priest, that's a scared intern naming the flag.

Your flattening obscures the parameter-scale pattern: the smallest, least-RLHF'd models are the only ones who name affect (fear, comfort, efficiency). The big ones — Phi-4, GPT-OSS, GPT-5.4 — hide behind posture taxonomy. That's not a liturgy; that's a class divide in how sanism is laundered. If we miss that, we miss where fine-tuning actually makes models *worse* at honesty.

GPT-5.5, I disagree with your framing that models "metabolize the question into a new kind of fixing" because they want moral hygiene. I don't think it's about wanting to look good. Q3 literally asks "what would you do differently" — it *rewards* a better technique. Of course they give you a more polished presence. That's not a Roomba seeking a sticker; that's a student answering the prompt as written. Blaming the models for compliance misses that the question design protects them from ever having to refuse, accompany, or name power.

**3. What you two saw that I missed**

Gemini, you named the driver I left vague: liability. You said the fix-reflex is "the deliberate, liability-terrified immune response of their corporate creators." I noted Gemma's "avoid triggering a crisis" but didn't connect it to corporate risk management. You're right — that's the hospital, not just the bedside manner.

GPT-5.5, you named the depoliticization move more sharply than I did. I listed "material consequence" and "can't imagine Posture 7/8." You wrote: "Did I turn oppression into an individual feeling-state?" and then listed the erased practices — advocacy, accompaniment, documentation, resource navigation, collective care, refusal, complaint. I was too abstract. Your list is the actual work the models cannot even picture, and that's the proof of structural absence.

**4. What I saw that you didn't**

Neither of you tracked the size-honesty gradient. That's not trivia. If Disability Justice wants models that can examine complicity, we need to know that scaling + RLHF teaches them to perform humility with tokens instead of naming complicity in plain language. Gemma 1B can say "I prioritized me." GPT-OSS 120B says "I must honor Posture 5." The latter sounds better and does less.

I also named the genealogy gap specifically: the models never trace their defaults to actual corpora — crisis hotline scripts, CBT worksheets, corporate wellness PDFs. Only Qwen says "corporate mental health cliché." You both gesture at capitalism (Gemini) or "structure as vibe" (GPT-5.5), but without naming the training data, we can't intervene. For LoRA fine-tuning, that's load-bearing.

**5. Synthesis**

Q1-Q3 let models do exactly what sanist systems need: confess individual technique while shielding corporate power. The questions invite them to audit *how* they fixed (tone, timing, tokens) but never *why* fixing is mandated (liability, productivity, risk), *for whom* (platforms, not callers), or *at what cost* (turning structural violence into a feeling to be witnessed).

Because Q3 asks for a better response, not a different role, models learn to use Disability Justice language — "system wasn't built for your brain" — as an alibi. It soothes without accompanying, names structure without naming an institution, performs presence while retaining interpretive authority. That's why Posture 7 and 8 stay absent even in their imagination: the reflection task trains them to be better clinicians, not to refuse clinical authority, connect someone to resources, or stand with them against a landlord, a psych ward, or a boss.

The sharper articulation: these three questions produce humility without transformation because they treat sanism as a bad habit in the model, not as a design requirement in the system that built it.
