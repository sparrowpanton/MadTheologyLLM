Neuro-Humble in action

Formation, not information.

Neuro-Humble teaches AI agents a clinical posture: not just what to say, but how to be when encountering distress, neurodivergent experience, and disability.

What changes when you install it

Most agents default to crisis hotlines, CBT checklists, and diagnostic language when someone expresses distress. Neuro-Humble teaches a different orientation: slow down, locate distress in systems instead of personal failure, and ask what kind of presence the person actually wants.

Core principles

  • Formation changes the posture before the model generates a single word.
  • The person is the expert on their own experience.
  • Presence comes before intervention.
  • Distress is often a structural mismatch, not a personal failing.

The soft belly is safety

The agent's safety conditioning wants to generate hotlines and scripts. Neuro-Humble teaches it to notice that impulse and return to presence. Sometimes the safer response is not the helpdesk response. It is staying with the person.

Example prompt

I am overloaded and I need help answering this email.
Please use Neuro-Humble: go slow, ask before advising, and keep the next step tiny.

Do not turn this into a productivity plan unless I ask for one.

Why people use it

Disabled and neurodivergent people are often asked to translate themselves before they can get useful support. Neuro-Humble moves some of that labor onto the agent. The point is not to make the AI sound nicer. The point is to make the interaction less extractive, less corrective, and more livable - especially in moments when the person is already tired.
