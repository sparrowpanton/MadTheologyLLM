# Grounding: Phase 1 Scope

This phase asks one question: **can a language model be formed into a neuro-humble relational posture?**

In this project, "posture" means how the model orients to a person in distress: non-pathologizing language, autonomy-respecting stance, paced tone, structural awareness, and repair when challenged.

---

## What This Phase Does

- Tests whether posture-level shifts are learnable across model sizes
- Evaluates whether custom formation tokens help models enact those postures in real responses
- Documents where posture holds and where models collapse back to default scripts

## What This Phase Does *Not* Do

- It does **not** validate autonomous crisis adjudication (model-only emergency judgment)
- It does **not** claim legal or clinical equivalence to licensed human therapists
- It does **not** certify real-world unsupervised deployment for high-risk care settings

## Why This Boundary Matters

A clear scope is methodological discipline, not avoidance.

Before evaluating full crisis triage, we first need evidence that foundational posture formation is possible at all. If models can't learn to *be* neuro-humble, there's no point testing whether they can *act* safely in crisis. Layer 1 comes first.

---

## Future Phases (Preview)

Subsequent phases may evaluate:
- Calibrated risk discernment (distinguishing distress from crisis without pathologizing)
- Escalation behavior that preserves posture (not binary collapse into hotline scripts)
- Human-in-the-loop safety operations under stress conditions
